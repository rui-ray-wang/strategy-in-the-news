## First-time setup (do this once)

1. Open the site
2. Click **🔑** in the top-right corner
3. Paste the token your instructor shared with you
4. You're ready — you won't need to do this again on the same browser/device

---

## Submitting your post

1. Fill in the article **headline, source, and date**
2. Start with what **caught your attention**, not a framework
3. Select one or two **lenses** from the buttons (a hint box will appear to guide your thinking)
4. Write your **analysis** — explain the logic behind the strategic actions in the news rather than just describing what happened
5. Write a **question the article left you thinking about** — something the news made you curious or uncertain about.
6. Click **Submit & Share →**

You'll see "Post submitted! ✓" when it goes through.

---

## Saving your post

Click **Preview post** to see a formatted version of your analysis, then click **Print / Save PDF** to save it to your computer.

---

## Reading peer posts

Click **👥 Peer Posts** at the top of the page to browse what your classmates have submitted. Click **"Read full analysis ↓"** on any card to expand the full post.

## Frameworks covered in this course

| Lens | Use when… |
|------|-----------|
| **External Environment** | A macro trend (political, economic, technological, regulatory) is driving the story |
| **Five Forces** | The news reshapes rivalry, entry barriers, supplier/buyer power, or substitutes |
| **RBV** | A firm's resource or capability is at stake — being built, eroded, or revealed |
| **Cost Leadership** | The firm is competing on scale, efficiency, or structural cost advantage |
| **Differentiation** | The firm is trying to stand apart — and this news strengthens or threatens that |
| **Focused Strategy** | The firm is targeting a specific segment others have overlooked |
| **Blue Ocean** | The firm is creating new demand rather than competing in an existing space |
